Destin Homa

Computer Engineer

My name is Destin Homa, and I've been building computers for nearly seven years. Looking back, I always had a certain fascination with computers, and how they ran. Once I began tinkering with my brother's old hand-me-down computer, there was no stopping me. My passion is building machines that can tackle any workload or game you throw at it, and I am proud of the unique skills I've developed for my given field. I am currently studying Political Science - Law and Society, with a minor in Business Administration and Accounting; I plan to use my eventual bachelors degree to pursue a career in the FBI.

For every employer I've worked for, I have displayed respectfulness, resourcefulness, and vigor in completing any job or task assigned to me. I worked previously as an eventual shift lead at Waba Grill in Wildomar, a chef for Farmer Boy's in Murrieta, and currently work at Home Depot in Murrieta where I have earned multiple awards, and am on track to being promoted to a Head Front-End Associate after my six months of dedicated work.

From a young age, my life was wrought with challenges. My sister and I took care of one another through tough times and various moves across California. I am the first of my family line to attend a four-year university, and I am proud of that fact. To me, any work worth doing is worth doing well, a motto I borrowed from my father. I want nothing more than to do the best I can in everything I can possibly do, whether that is building a desktop, writing a paper, or doing simple day-to-day tasks.
